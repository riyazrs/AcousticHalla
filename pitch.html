Lessons Learned

  • Constraint Breeds Creativity: Forcing seemingly random parameters (panda, piano, lime, mountain, South Korea) generated a genuinely novel product vertical (sensory tech for wildlife).
  • Research Grounds Fiction: Transitioning from a whimsical story to a rigorous academic question validated the commercial viability of the idea.
  • Hardware is Hard: Designing the MVP timeline highlighted the massive friction in building weather-proof edge devices for mega-fauna.
  • Design as a Differentiator: In the B2B conservation space, high-end aesthetics instantly separate a serious startup from a hobbyist project.
  • Data is the True Product: While the hardware is cool, the recurring SaaS revenue comes from the automated welfare compliance reports.
  • Brevity is Powerful: Stripping the pitch deck to single-statement slides forces absolute clarity of thought and maximizes audience retention.
