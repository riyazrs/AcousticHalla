# 03 Problem Statement

**Diagnosis:** 
Animal sanctuaries, large-scale zoological conservation centres, and public wildlife reserves face a pervasive crisis: "zoochosis," or severe captivity-induced stress and behavioral depression in endangered, highly intelligent mammals. Current environmental enrichment programs—typically consisting of basic foraging puzzles or static toys—fail to provide the complex cognitive and multi-sensory engagement these animals require. This chronic under-stimulation leads to elevated cortisol levels, repetitive pacing, a 30% decrease in reproductive success, and compromised physical health.

**Target Segment:** 
High-tier B2B zoological institutions, government-funded conservation parks, and private wildlife sanctuaries (specifically targeting those managing large, intelligent mammal species like primates, bears, and elephants) that are under increasing pressure from animal welfare regulators to modernise their cognitive enrichment protocols.
